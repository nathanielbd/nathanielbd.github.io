cold crisp catnap
High school project
A sarcastic interaction with the first-person narrator

My first experience with programming was in middle school, creating a Flash game aggregator website with HTML/CSS/Javascript. While I later took down the site and lost the jump drive, it would be over three years until I found myself interested in computers again and created 'cold crisp catnap.'

The webgame is a tribute to the Zork franchise, which hold some of the best of the old Infocom classic text adventures. I wanted to capture the thrill of out-smarting the Ravenous Bugblatter Beast of Traal (however easy it may be), the thrill of routing the Wizard of Frobozz, and the dread of being eaten by a grue into a game I could call my own.

It's not terribly well-written; the core logic is all in client-side Javascript, the text parsing isn't done in any clever way like any DSL or compiler would function, and the game just doesn't have the same witty, fantastical feel as the originals. However, it holds a special place in my heart as my first complete application where I had to teach myself everything from scratch.

The Sick Shrimp Project
24-hour Hackathon
The landing page for the webapp

The Sick Shrimp project was created during MinneHack, the largest hackathon in the Twin Cities. During a span of 24-hours, I led a team of 3 to produce a webapp for Cargill's prompt to make aquaculture farmers' lives easier.

The webapp employs a convolutional neural network to classify images of shrimp as either 'health' or 'infected' by the White Spot Virus for automated diagnosis. The webapp also asks the farmers for information about their shrimps' growing conditions so that we can curate a dataset for further investigation into the causes and factors of the disease.

CSBIO Lab
Volunteer Research
Genetic interaction network of the human genome
Spacial analysis of functional enrichment (SAFE) of the global interaction network above

I volunteered my time at the CSBIO lab under PI Dr. Chad Myers for a semester during my first undergraduate year at the University of Minnesota. The lab focuses on computational biology and functional genomics.

I wrote R scripts to find patterns of genetic interactions in CRISPR-edited HAP1 cells. I visualized the data in the shown figures using Cytoscape.
